Planets of our Solar System

Our Solar System has eight major planets. The four inner planets are small and rocky. The four outer planets are much larger and mostly made of gas or ice.

Mercury

Type: Rocky

Fun fact: Mercury has almost no atmosphere, so its surface gets extremely hot and cold.

Venus

Type: Rocky

Fun fact: Venus is hotter than Mercury because of its thick, heat-trapping atmosphere.

Earth

Type: Rocky

Fun fact: Earth is the only known planet with liquid water and complex life.

Mars

Type: Rocky

Fun fact: Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons.

Jupiter

Type: Gas giant

Fun fact: Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has lasted for centuries.

Saturn

Type: Gas giant

Fun fact: Saturn's rings are made of ice and rock.

Uranus

Type: Ice giant

Fun fact: Uranus rotates on its side, so its poles point almost directly at the Sun.

Neptune

Type: Ice giant

Fun fact: Neptune has some of the strongest winds in the Solar System.
